# Terminal

The **Terminal** is the main Siren screen. It connects **prediction markets** and **tokens** in one place.

---

## Live signal feed

The left rail is a shared live feed for **Kalshi** and **Polymarket**.

Each signal card shows:

- **Source badge**: Kalshi or Polymarket
- **Question**
- **Probability delta**
- **Timestamp**
- **Linked token count**
- **Volume context**

Use the top filter to toggle:

- **ALL**
- **KALSHI**
- **POLYMARKET**

Siren also shows per-source connection health so one market venue failing does not take down the full terminal.

---

## Markets

Below the signal rail is the broader market browser.

You can:

- Filter by category
- Search by keyword
- Open a market from either rail
- Jump straight into the token surface beneath it

---

## Market detail

The selected market card is the center of the terminal.

It shows:

- Full market text
- Venue rail
- Current YES / NO probability
- Velocity and liquidity
- Market metadata
- A branded **share / download** export action

This is the social object too. The exported image is branded with `onsiren.xyz`, so the same market view can travel cleanly to X, Telegram, or group chats.

---

## Linked tokens

For each market, Siren surfaces relevant tokens by:

- Matching market language to token names, tickers, and descriptions
- Querying the Solana meme universe through DexScreener
- Prioritizing launch-ready narratives when a market clearly wants its own token

Each token row shows:

- Token name + symbol
- Price and 24h change
- Liquidity / FDV (when available)
- Risk context and launchpad metadata when available

From this panel you can open the buy / sell panel and execute the token leg directly in Siren.

---

## Execution notes

- **Kalshi** powers the prediction market rail in Siren today.
- **Polymarket** is live as a parallel signal and market-context source.
- **Jupiter** powers token swaps on the Solana side.

This keeps the core workflow tight:

1. Detect the event move
2. Open the market
3. Sweep the linked Solana token flow
4. Share the market card or move into launch mode

---

## Access gating

In production, the Terminal is gated behind a **6‑digit access code**. This is configured on the API and web env via:

- `SIREN_ACCESS_CODE` (API)
- `SIREN_GATE_ENABLED` (web)

See the [Quickstart](../quickstart) page for how users get access.
